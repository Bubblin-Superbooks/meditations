  2. To Nature, which gives and again resumes all things, the well-instructed, modest man will say: Give what thou wilt; take again what thou wilt. And this he says, not with ostentation, but out of pure obedience and good will to Nature.
  3. What remains to you of this life is little. Live as on a mountain. For it makes no difference whether we live here or there, provided we live like citizens everywhere in the world. Let men see and know you as a man indeed, living according to Nature. If they cannot endure you, let them slay you. It is better so than to live as they live.
