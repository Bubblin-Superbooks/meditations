1. Consider well the nature of things, distinguishing between matter, cause, and purpose.
  2. What a glorious power is given to man, never to do any action of which God will not approve, and to welcome whatever God appoints for him!
  3. As to what happens in the course of nature, the Gods are not to be blamed. They never do wrong, willingly or unwillingly. Neither are men to be blamed, for they do no wrong willingly. There is therefore none to blame.
  4. How ridiculous, and how like a foreigner, is he who is surprised at anything which happens in life!
