First of all, tragedy was introduced to remind us that certain events happen, and are fated to happen as they do; and to teach us that what entertains us on the stage should not grieve us on the greater stage of the world. You see that such things must be accomplished; and that even they bore them who cried aloud, O Cithaeron! Our dramatic poets have said some excellent things; especially the following:— Me and my children, if the Gods neglect, It is for some good reason— and again, Vain is all anger at external things; and, To reap our life like ears of ripened corn— and the like.
