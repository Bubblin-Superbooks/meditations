1. long for a child that may no longer be yours.
  2. Epictetus said that, when you kiss your child, you should whisper within yourself: To-morrow perhaps he may die. Ill-omened words! say you. The words have no evil omen, says he, but simply indicate an act of Nature. Is it of evil omen to say the corn is reaped?
  3. The green grape, the ripe cluster, the dried grape are all changes, not into nothing, but into that which is not at present.
  4. No man can rob you of your liberty of action; as has been said by Epictetus.
  5. He tells us also that we must find out the true art of assenting; and in treating of our impulses he says that we must be vigilant in restraining them, that they may act with proper reservation, with public spirit, with due sense of proportion; also that we should refrain utterly from sensual passion; and not be restive in matters where we have no control.
